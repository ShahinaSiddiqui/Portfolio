Interactive E-Learning Module on Home Loan Concepts

Project Brief:

The requirement was to design an e-learning module that trained frontline employees on the essentials of home loans. The module needed to simplify technical aspects such as eligibility of the customer and property, loan variants, LTV (Loan-to-Value ratio) and FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income ratio) formulas, the surrogate programme, and documentation. It also included guidance on handling client objections, with the ultimate goal of increasing home loan sales by enabling employees to confidently explain and pitch products to clients.

Conducting Need Analysis:

I began with a detailed needs analysis to understand both the content challenges and the business goals. The discussions explored:

  • What employees struggled with most? (from interpreting dense policy charts to explaining formulas and documentation to clients)
  • What would help them retain and apply this information in real conversations?

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis highlighted several key insights that shaped the design of the module:

  • Employees found existing resources (charts, PDFs, and detailed documents) too complex to digest or explain to clients.
  • There was a need to break down heavy material into small, structured, and easy-to-absorb chunks, supported by quick-reference job aids.
  • Complex concepts such as the surrogate programme and FOIR/LTV formulas had to be simplified and presented visually.
  • Objection handling was identified as crucial to prepare employees for real client conversations.
  • The design needed to focus on reducing cognitive load, using interactive elements and a conversational tone to make learning accessible to all levels of staff.

Audience :

Sales personnel handling home loan enquiries and sales

Module Duration

30 minutes (self-paced)

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design
  • Content Curation and Transformation (Redesigning dense charts and PDFs into visual, interactive elements with downloadable references.)
  • Authoring & Development on Articulate storyline 360

Learning Design Strategy:

The design strategy was anchored in Cognitive Load Theory, ensuring heavy material was simplified and delivered in manageable portions.

  • I began with curating the content and broke down complex concepts into short, digestible segments to make learning more accessible.
  • To bring a high level of interactivity, I built in flashcards, hotspots, and bite-sized videos that reinforced key points while keeping learners engaged.
  • Using the Dual Coding Theory, I paired visuals with text so formulas and criteria became clearer and easier to recall. This helped greatly to simplify technical details.
  • I integrated the VAK Theory to reach diverse learner preferences. Charts and graphics were used for visual learners, narrations and scripts for auditory learners, and clickable scenarios for kinesthetic learners.
  • To simulate real client interactions, I created objection-handling practice activities that allowed employees to rehearse conversations in a safe environment.

The Outcome

The module was very well received for its simplicity and clear takeaways. Team leaders and sales managers observed improved retention, while learners appreciated the downloadable references as practical on-the-job aids.
