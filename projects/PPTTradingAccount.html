Training PPT: 3-in-1 Trading Account

Project Brief:

Following the success of the Game-Based module on "Stock market concepts and 3-in-1 Trading Account", the client requested the creation of a Training PPT to be used in the training sessions.

Conducting Need Analysis:

Keeping in mind the client’s requirement, I began with a need analysis to understand the use and scope of the PPT. Questions explored included

  • Would the PPT be used for internal trainers only, or also external facilitators?
  • Would it support purely synchronous classroom sessions, or could it also extend to blended formats?
  • What duration should the PPT be designed for?
  • What specific activities or sections should it include — e.g., profiling case studies, role plays, or objection handling?
  • What level of trainer guidance would be required to ensure consistency in delivery?

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis confirmed that the PPT would be used by both internal and external trainers. It was designed to support synchronous classroom training (with potential for adaptation into blended use if needed). The duration was finalized at 2 hours, giving trainers enough time for product coverage as well as interactive activities.

The analysis also confirmed that trainer notes were essential, so facilitators could deliver consistent, activity-oriented sessions regardless of their background or style.

Audience :

Sales employees responsible for engaging with clients and pitching trading accounts

Module Duration

2 hours classroom session

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design
  • PPT Creation & Visual Design
  • Content Structuring
  • Creative Coordination (icons, layouts, and visuals)

Learning Design Strategy:

The design strategy was guided by principles of impactful presentation design and classroom engagement.

  • Slides were designed with minimal text and strong visuals, ensuring focus on the trainer’s facilitation rather than slide reading. I applied clear structure, consistent typography, and icons to make complex ideas easier to scan and recall.
  • Detailed trainer notes were added below slides, giving cues for facilitation, stories, and timing, so delivery remained consistent across trainers.
  • I designed role plays, case studies, and profiling exercises into the PPT, making learning hands-on rather than passive.
  • Dedicated slides included common objections and sample responses, which trainers could use for quick role play or guided discussion.
  • Interactive prompts, open-ended discussion slides, and scenario-based questions kept participants active throughout the session.
  • A blended approach of knowledge checks and application-based exercises reinforced learning before closing.

The Outcome

The Training PPT helped transform trading account classroom sessions into structured, interactive experiences. Trainers reported that the detailed notes and built-in activities made facilitation easier, more engaging, and more impactful. The client was pleased to hear this positive feedback and particularly appreciated how the PPT ensured consistency with the earlier e-learning module, creating a seamless learning journey.
