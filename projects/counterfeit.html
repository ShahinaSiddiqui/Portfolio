Counterfeit Currency: Game-Based E-Learning Module

Project Brief:

The client required a game-based e-learning module to train tellers on detecting counterfeit Indian currency. Since accuracy in counterfeit detection is critical for compliance and customer trust, the training needed to be highly practice-oriented and designed to sustain engagement in a technical subject.

Conducting Need Analysis:

I began with a detailed needs analysis to align the design with teller responsibilities and business goals. Questions explored included:

  • Which denominations and their security features should be coverd in the module?
  • Would employees be comfortable with game mechanics being embedded in the training experience?
  • Should the assessment be designed as a standalone quiz or integrated within the gameplay flow?
  • What was the expected duration of the module?

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis confirmed that all key denominations and their security features had to be included, employees would be comfortable with game mechanics if kept intuitive, the assessment should be embedded in the gameplay flow, and the duration was finalized at around one hour to balance practice with teller schedules.

In addition, it was clear that tellers needed repeated hands-on practice in identifying counterfeits. Standard quizzes or static training would not suffice; a story-driven, immersive approach was necessary. To make the assessment realistic, the module also had to replicate actual teller practices such as placing genuine notes in a safe and shredding counterfeits.

Audience :

Tellers and cash-handling staff of the bank

Module Duration

1 hour (self-paced)

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design
  • Gamification & Learning Strategy
  • Content Curation (denomination security features, RBI standards, teller practices)
  • Creative Coordination (custom visuals, storyline design, UI/UX flow)
  • Authoring & Development

Learning Design Strategy:

The solution was to merge popular gaming mechanics with real teller tasks, creating an immersive, practice-heavy learning journey.

  • Modeled on the popular web series Money Heist, the storyline cast the learner as the head of a currency printing operation. Each denomination could only be “printed” after exploring its security features and metallic seals.
  • Learners studied notes through hotspots and visual cues, focusing on watermarks, microtext, metallic threads, and color shifts.
  • During printing, counterfeiters attacked the process. Learners had to defend the system, adding excitement while reinforcing focus on features.
  • As part of the final challenge, learners tested corrupted notes — dragging genuine notes into a safe and counterfeits into a shredder, mimicking real teller actions.
  • Each level increased in difficulty, just as counterfeit detection in real life requires sharper observation under pressure.
  • I incorporated instant corrective feedback into each interaction, reinforcing why a note was genuine or fake.
  • Dense RBI material was simplified into layered, bite-sized learning, woven into the gameplay instead of static reading.

To bring this concept to life, I:

  • Designed and created the design document, curating content mapped to learning objectives while minimizing cognitive load.
  • Developed a detailed storyboard showing step-by-step progression of game elements, content flow, interactivities, and knowledge checks.
  • Created visual mock-ups to align game mechanics with engaging design elements.
  • Authored and published the complete module as SCORM-compliant packages for seamless deployment on the client’s LMS.

The Outcome

The module was a flagship success, widely praised by both learners and the client. Tellers found it engaging, realistic, and confidence-building, reporting that the gameplay gave them practice they had never received in classroom sessions. The client valued how a compliance-heavy topic was turned into a fun, immersive experience, driving high participation across branches. They also highly appreciated the clean, well-structured design, which made the module both effective and visually appealing.
