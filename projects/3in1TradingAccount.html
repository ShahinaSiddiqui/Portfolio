Game-Based Stock Market Simulation for the Trading Account

Project Brief:

The requirement was to create an e-learning module that trained employees on their 3-in-1 trading account. The purpose was to give employees deeper insights into stock market trading, so they could pitch the product more confidently and generate stronger sales outcomes.

Conducting Need Analysis:

I began with a detailed needs analysis to uncover the learning and business requirements. This included questions around employees’ familiarity with trading concepts, the level of complexity they could handle, and what type of learning design would sustain engagement with such a heavy subject.

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis revealed that employees needed clarity on how to sell and explain the 3-in-1 trading account confidently and required exposure to real-life trading scenarios that mirrored challenges they faced, such as managing aftermarket orders during morning meetings. It also became evident that employees had to understand how to link client goals with appropriate investment products and create interest around advisory services. Since the subject matter was heavy, the content had to be delivered in a conversational, flowing manner with simulation elements to ensure even those with minimal prior knowledge could grasp it easily. Engagement was a priority, so the module needed to be interactive, game-driven, and immersive to sustain participation across the target group., progressive disclosure, and see-then-do practice.

Audience :

Frontline employees at branch level, directly interacting with clients

Module Duration

1 hour (self-paced)

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design: Designed the module structure to blend game mechanics with trading concepts.
  • Content Curation: Structured the simulation and adapted authentic product details, formulas, and scenarios into learner-friendly flows.
  • Creative Coordination: Managed gameplay elements such as avatars, NPC(Non Playing Character) rival, and goal-based progression.
  • Authoring & Development: Built the simulation end-to-end in the authoring tool(articulate storyline 360), integrating interactivity, scenarios, and assessments.

Learning Design Strategy:

The learning design strategy merged gameplay with practice-oriented learning to simplify stock market concepts and keep learners engaged:

  • Learners played against an NPC rival, with success measured by outperforming the system in trading.
  • Gamification techniques like avatars, scoring, goals, and progression were used to enhance motivation.
  • Even while designing the game-based simulation, I remained vigilant about reducing cognitive load and ensured that the dense content was broken into smaller, flowing segments with a conversational tone.
  • I created scenario-driven design to replicated everyday stock market trading realities, such as aftermarket orders and missed trades.
  • I used VAK theory to ensure that visual learners engaged with dashboards, charts, and trading visuals, auditory learners benefited from advisory prompts and voiceovers and kinesthetic learners interacted through decision-making, branching, and clicks.

The Outcome

The module was very well received. Participation levels were high, with employees showing enthusiasm for learning through a simulation game. Feedback highlighted how the experience made complex trading concepts simple, interactive, and fun. Most importantly, employees gained confidence in pitching the 3-in-1 trading account, and the training contributed to improved sales conversations and stronger product uptake. The client was so impressed with the success of this module and the feedback it generated that they also asked me to create a training PPT for use in in-house classroom sessions.
