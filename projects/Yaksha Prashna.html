Yaksha Prashna: Mythology-Inspired Ethics Assessment

Project Brief:

The client wanted an assessment module on ethics that moved beyond standard quizzes and engaged employees in meaningful reflection. They specifically requested that the design be drawn from Indian mythology.

Conducting Need Analysis:

Keeping in mind the client requirements, I thought to use one of the episodes from the Mahabharata, the "Yaksha Prashna". In this story, the Pandavas were wandering in exile when they came upon a lake guarded by a Yaksha (a mystical being). The Yaksha challenged them: no one could drink the water unless they answered his questions. One by one, the Pandavas ignored the warning and drank, falling lifeless, until only Yudhishthir remained. He patiently engaged with the Yaksha, answering a series of deep moral and philosophical questions. Impressed by his wisdom and integrity, the Yaksha restored the lives of his brothers. This mythological framework created a powerful parallel to workplace dilemmas, showing that right choices, guided by values, lead to the best outcomes. With this thought in mind, I started with the need analysis and began with questions such as:

  • What kinds of ethical dilemmas do employees commonly face in their day-to-day roles?
  • Which organizational values should the assessment reinforce most strongly?
  • I also pitched the idea of using the Yaksha Prashna episode, discussing with the client how a mythological story could be mapped to workplace ethical dilemmas and adapted into an assessment framework.

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis confirmed that the Yaksha Prashna framework was highly relevant and effective for this module. The assessment was supposed to include structured yet reflective questions that allowed employees to practice ethical decision-making in a meaningful way. The mythological parallel reinforced that right choices, even under pressure, lead to better outcomes; just as Yudhishthir’s wisdom restored his brothers. To make this relevant for employees, the tone had to remain conversational yet thought-provoking, encouraging them to pause and reflect on their own choices rather than rush through answers. Progression also needed to feel accountable, with advancement tied to answering questions thoughtfully and receiving feedback aligned with ethical expectations.

Audience :

Corporate staff

Module Duration

30–40 minutes

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design – created design documents, storyboards, scripts, and assessment
  • Assessment & Learning Strategy – designed the assessmnet and feedback mechanism to blend with the flow of the story
  • Content Curation – researched, structured, and aligned the questions with the learning objectives
  • Creative Coordination – managed visuals, 2D illustrations and animation for impactful aesthetics
  • Authoring & Development – built SCORM-compliant assessment module on Articulate Storyline 360 and Rise 360

Learning Design Strategy:

The design strategy focused on turning a simple set of assessment questions into a powerful, story-driven experience.

  • Questions were presented as real workplace dilemmas, allowing employees to reflect and respond as if answering on Yudhishthir’s behalf.
  • Advancement was tied to answering thoughtfully so that the learners could move forward only after responding, reinforcing ownership of their decisions.
  • Immediate, value-based feedback was provided after each response, guiding learners to align with ethical expectations rather than just scoring right or wrong.
  • A conversational yet thought-provoking tone was maintained throughout, ensuring learners felt engaged rather than tested.

The design strategy focused on turning a simple set of assessment questions into a powerful, story-driven experience.

The Outcome

The module successfully transformed a traditional ethics assessment into an immersive mythology-based experience. The client praised the innovation of using mythology in assessment, and high participation combined with positive feedback.
