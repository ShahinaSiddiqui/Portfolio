Vikram–Betal Inspired Decision-Making Assessment

Project Brief:

The client wanted an assessment module on decision-making in ethical dilemmas, anchored in Indian mythology. The objective was to help frontline employees reflect on everyday dilemmas and practice choosing the right course of action.

Conducting Need Analysis:

To shape the module effectively, I began with a detailed needs analysis. Through discussions with stakeholders, I sought to understand:

  • What kinds of ethical gray areas and dilemmas employees typically face in their day-to-day work.
  • How employees currently make decisions, and where they might struggle to apply organizational values.
  • The constraints of time and the delivery format most suitable for the target audience.
  • The desired business outcomes-strengthening a culture of doing the right thing, and ensuring client trust through value-driven decisions.

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The need analysis helped to define the practical parameters:

  • Learning outcomes: enable employees to pause, reflect, and make the right choices when confronted with dilemmas
  • Business outcomes: reinforce ethical decision-making as a core behavior, minimizing risks and building credibility with clients

    • Audience :

    Frontline staff dealing with frequent decision-making dilemmas

    Module Duration

    20 minutes (self-paced)

    My Expertise in Action:

    • Instructional Design
    • Learning Flow Design
    • Creative coordination with 2D illustrator and animator
    • Authoring & development of the module on articulate storyline 360

    Learning Design Strategy:

    The strategy blended mythology with behavioral assessment, ensuring a unique and memorable experience:

    • The Vikram–Betal story acted as the central spine of the assessment with custom 2D animations and illustrations that created an immersive experience.
    • Each question represented a real-world ethical or business dilemma.
    • Learners progressed only by making choices and seeing how they aligned with ethical expectations.
    • Betal’s questioning was designed to make learners pause and reflect on values and decision-making.

    The Outcome

    The module was highly appreciated by the client for creatively combining mythology with modern-day workplace dilemmas. Learners found it engaging and thought-provoking, with the story format making it far more memorable than a traditional ethics assessment.
