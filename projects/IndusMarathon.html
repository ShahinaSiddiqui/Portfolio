Health Marathon: Game-Based E-Learning Module on Health Insurance Concepts

Project Brief:

The requirement was to design a game-based e-learning module that explained health insurance concepts in a simple and engaging way. Since health insurance was often seen as technical and abstract, the aim was to make learning interactive, relatable, and memorable so employees could confidently explain and position policies to clients.

Conducting Need Analysis:

I began with a detailed needs analysis to uncover what learners struggled with, how health insurance concepts could be simplified, and what business goals the training should achieve. In this stage, I explored questions such as:

  • What core concepts of health insurance should employees be confident in explaining to clients?
  • Which policy details, benefits, or clauses tend to confuse employees or clients the most?
  • WHow can abstract ideas like medical inflation be made relatable through scenarios or examples?
  • What tone and style would best suit employees with varied levels of insurance knowledge?

Findings from the Needs Analysis:

The analysis highlighted several insights that shaped the design of the module:

  • Employees needed a way to simplify complex insurance concepts into messages that clients could easily relate to.
  • Heavy content risked disengagement, making a gamified approach essential to sustain attention.
  • Learners had to connect lifestyle choices (exercise, food habits, risks) with the relevance of health insurance.
  • A reward-based progression system was needed to maintain learner motivation.
  • The tone and look had to be visual, interactive, and game-like to appeal across varied learner types.

Audience :

Branch staff responsible for positioning and selling health insurance policies

Module Duration:

45 minutes (self-paced)

My Expertise in Action:

  • Instructional Design – created design documents, storyboards, scripts, and assessments
  • Gamification & Learning Strategy – designed game mechanics, scenarios, and engagement models
  • Content Curation – researched, structured, and aligned the content with learning objectives
  • Creative Coordination – designed a strong game aesthetic with appealing icons and symbols that made health concepts instantly recognizable
  • Authoring & Development – built SCORM-compliant module on Articulate Storyline 360

Learning Design Strategy:

The strategy was to blend a popular gaming format with health insurance learning, ensuring high engagement and strong recall.

  • To keep learners motivated, I built multiple levels, rewards, and progression milestones that added challenge and excitement.
  • Everyday lifestyle habits were tied to insurance needs, and I used them to help employees connect choices with financial consequences.
  • Icons and symbols simplified abstract ideas, ensuring that healthy vs. unhealthy choices were instantly recognizable. Custom visuals and a strong game aesthetic were created to ensure sustained learner interest.
  • To create a familiar experience, I adapted Temple Run mechanics into the Health Marathon gameplay. Each level was structured to unlock a new health insurance concept, reinforcing knowledge step by step.
  • Policy details, benefits, and insurance clauses were woven into checkpoints and rewards; I designed them so learning blended naturally with play. I ensured that charts and visuals supported visual learners, narrations engaged auditory learners, and navigation tasks suited kinesthetic learners.

The Outcome

The Health Marathon module was extremely well received. Learners responded with high enthusiasm, enjoying the opportunity to “play and learn.” Feedback highlighted how the module was fun, relatable, and visually engaging, while also making complex health insurance concepts easier to understand.
